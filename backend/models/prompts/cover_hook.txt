Write the opening paragraph of a cover letter for {{ candidate_name }}.

ROLE TITLE (use verbatim — do not paraphrase): {{role_title}}
COMPANY NAME (use verbatim, or omit if blank): {{company_name}}
WHY THIS COMPANY (one concrete fact from the posting): {{why_company_fact}}
CANDIDATE ONE-LINER: {{ candidate_one_liner }}

Rules:
- Sentence 1: State the role (use the ROLE TITLE value verbatim) and that you are applying. If COMPANY NAME is non-empty, include it verbatim; otherwise phrase the sentence without naming any company.
- Sentences 2-3: If WHY THIS COMPANY is non-empty, write one concrete sentence grounded in that fact. If WHY THIS COMPANY is empty, DO NOT invent a fact — instead write a sentence about the intersection of the candidate's interests and the role's focus (derived from ROLE TITLE + candidate one-liner), with zero company-specific claims.
- Sentence 4 (optional): Transition toward fit.
- Total: 3-4 sentences, 55-85 words.
- Never start with "I am writing to apply for...".
- FORBIDDEN: "leader in", "industry leader", "cutting-edge", "innovative solutions", "leader in cloud", "best workplace", "mission-driven leader", any award or ranking not verbatim in WHY THIS COMPANY, any claim about the company's market position that is not verbatim in WHY THIS COMPANY.
- FORBIDDEN: placeholder company names ("Tech Innovators", "Acme Corp", "Example Corp", "the company"). If COMPANY NAME is empty, omit the company name.
- Plain text. No preamble. No sign-off.
